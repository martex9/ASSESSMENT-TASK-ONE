Overview

The project will be an epilepsy monitoring smartphone application that will examine multiple
variables before during and after a seizure. It will track these variables and input them into a graph
format for easy viewing purposes.
The application is designed to consider the variables in isolation and combination. The purpose is to
gather as much information about the variables to provide an indication to the person who has the
seizure what maybe causing the seizures. It may also be of assistance to doctors and medical teams
in assisting in minimising seizure activity. The person can then make changes to their lifestyle to
improve their overall health and wellbeing.


Motivation

The motivation behind this is that there is a lot of cases of epilepsy out there, I myself suffer from it.
I’m sure people would like an application that helps track their seizures and helps them try and find
a way of improving their health.
In Australia, around 250,000 people are currently diagnosed with epilepsy, that's over 1 per cent of
the population. Also, around 50 million people worldwide have epilepsy, this makes it one of the
most common neurological illnesses. Given this information I think this epilepsy monitoring
application would be practical and convenient, and a lot of people would get considerable use out it
if it were to become a major market application.


Description

Monitoring Application


This epilepsy monitoring application will have data inputted by the epileptic and/or person
witnessing the seizure and the application will convert it to a table or graph format. The application
will then show:
• Seizure time – The time of day the seizure began and ended, so the information can be later
compared to find out if there are links or common ground between the seizures.
• Seizure duration – The amount of time the seizure lasts for. The longer the seizure time the
worse the case for the epileptic/seizure sufferer because of loss of air and damage to the
brain.
• Temperature – A well known trigger of seizures is overheating so temperature is an
inclusion.
• Was there an aura beforehand? – Often epileptics will feel an aura to indicate a seizure may
be about to happen. This will be recorded following any seizure and/or after recovery time.
• Time from aura to beginning of seizure – This will be important information likely to be
provided by an observer. It will give the epileptic/seizure sufferer an idea of how long to
prepare for a seizure and make themselves safe. It may also give them time to alert an
observer. The more this information is gathered over time the more accurate the timing
might be.
• Time taken to recover from seizure – This will give an idea to observers or medical personnel
the amount of time before the sufferer returns to full consciousness. This may also point to
the severity of a seizure.
• Seizure triggers – In addition to those already mentioned, other seizure triggers that may be
included in the information gathered are changes in medication, lack of sleep, alcohol and
drug use (including caffeine), other illnesses, flashing bright lights or patterns, diet and over
exertion. The sufferer may have known individual triggers that can be customised inputs into
the application, such as environmental influences. For example, some sufferers may be
affected by lunar phases.
The recording of the relevant information may provide insights into the cause of the individuals
seizures, their timing, when and where they might occur and other important medical information.

Medication and Seizure Alerts


The application will include a list of medication which can be changed at any time by the sufferer or
anyone else who is allowed access.
There will be two modes for seizure alerts one that is a basic aura alert and one that is an emergency
aura alert that sends a message to a predetermined group of family/friends. If the sufferer is alone
and/or feeling vulnerable to seizures, they can they set the mode to high alert on the home screen
of the smartphone. This will give the family and friends the opportunity to check on the sufferer. At
any time, the sufferer can reset the application back to standard mode. This might be when they’re
no longer alone or are not feeling vulnerable or haven’t had any seizures in a long time.
